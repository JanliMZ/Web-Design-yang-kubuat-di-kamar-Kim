About 750 m long, Church Street in Bangalore is one of the busiest and most beloved streets, named after St. Mark’s Cathedral. It is lined with gift shops, music shops, bookstores, restaurants and cafes, some of them being the oldest in the city, all selling a wide variety of products and tasty treats. There are days when artists set up shop out on the streets and sell their work that includes paintings, photo frames, posters and handmade items. There are arcades for children and adults to play at and tattoo parlours too.

How to reach Church Street: There are several metro lines that go to Church Street; the Mahatma Gandhi Road Metro Station (purple line), Whitefield Metro Station (purple line), MG Road Metro Station (yellow line) etc. are all located within 1km of the Street. Church Street owes its name to St. Mark’s Cathedral, an architectural masterpiece dating back to the 19th century. Built in the Neo-Gothic style, this cathedral is an enduring symbol of Bangalore’s colonial past. The street was christened Church Street as it leads to this magnificent cathedral, showcasing its historical prominence. Banglore, in the past, was known as the “Garden City of India ”. It was surrounded by lush greenery and a pleasant climate. The British designed Church Street as a picturesque promenade flanked by majestic trees and gardens. Today, remnants of this history are visible through old colonial bungalows and elegant architecture that line the street.
