Amit Shah accuses Bihar government of 'appeasement politics', predicts 40 seats for BJP in LS polls

Shah said that the caste survey deliberately exaggerated the Muslim and Yadav population as part of 'appeasement politics' and emphasised that decision to conduct the caste survey was made while the Nitish Kumar's JD(U) was a part of NDA.

Union Home Minister Amit Shah on Sunday, November 5, delivered a scathing attack at Bihar's Nitish Kumar government, saying that the caste survey deliberately exaggerated the Muslim and Yadav population as part of 'appeasement politics'. He emphasized that the decision to conduct the caste survey was made while the Nitish Kumar's JD(U) was a part of NDA.

"The alliance partners (Grand Alliance) call themselves the well-wishers of backward classes. I want to tell them to not fall for what they say. The decision to conduct the caste survey in Bihar was taken when the JD(U) was part of the NDA. But the way the survey was conducted reveals the ulterior design of the Mahagathbandhan government... population of Muslims and Yadavas were deliberately inflated during the exercise," he said, while addressing a public rally in Bihar's Muzaffarpur.

He alleged the Mahagathbandhan government of indulging in politics of appeasement and not being interested in the welfare of people belonging to other backward communities.
